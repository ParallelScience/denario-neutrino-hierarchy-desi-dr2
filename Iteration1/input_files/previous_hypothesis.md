The current Bayesian evidence framework assumes a fixed, rigid mapping between the cosmological parameter $\Sigma m_\nu$ and the neutrino mass eigenstates, effectively treating the oscillation parameters as nuisance parameters with fixed central values. I hypothesize that the "decisive" evidence for the Normal Hierarchy (NH) is artificially inflated by the assumption of fixed oscillation parameters (NuFIT 6.0). By performing a joint Bayesian inference that treats the oscillation parameters ($\Delta m^2_{21}, \Delta m^2_{31}$) as stochastic variables with their own probability distributions (incorporating the full covariance matrices from NuFIT 6.0), we can quantify the "oscillation-induced uncertainty" in the Bayes factor $K$. I propose to compute the marginal likelihood by integrating over the full oscillation parameter space, rather than using point estimates. This will test whether the hierarchy preference is robust against the current experimental uncertainties in the mass-squared splittings, particularly given that the IH minimum mass is sensitive to the precise value of $\Delta m^2_{32}$. This approach will provide a more conservative and statistically rigorous lower bound on $K$, revealing if the "decisive" status holds when the full experimental error budget of neutrino physics is propagated into the cosmological model comparison.
