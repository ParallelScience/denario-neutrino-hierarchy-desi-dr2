The current analysis is technically sound and provides a compelling, well-documented case for the Normal Hierarchy (NH). However, to elevate this from a computational exercise to a definitive scientific contribution, the following critical gaps must be addressed:

1. **Address the "Prior-Data Conflict" explicitly:** The analysis notes that the cosmological posterior is centered at $\mu_0 = -0.009$ eV (or $-0.036$ eV for FC). This implies a significant portion of the likelihood is truncated at $\Sigma m_\nu = 0$. You must perform a "prior-data conflict" test (e.g., the Box p-value or a posterior predictive check). If the data is pushing the posterior into the unphysical region, the Bayes factor is sensitive to the *shape* of the truncation. You should report how much of the evidence is driven by the "edge" of the prior vs. the bulk of the likelihood.

2. **Refine the Dark Energy Sensitivity:** The drop in the HS Bayes factor from 464.6 ($\Lambda$CDM) to 42.6 ($w_0w_a$CDM) is the most important result for robustness. You currently treat this as a "degradation." You should instead frame this as a **"Model-Hierarchy Degeneracy"**. Quantify the correlation between $\Sigma m_\nu$ and $w_a$ in the DESI chains. If the hierarchy preference vanishes when $w_a$ is free, the "decisive" claim is fragile. You should explicitly state: "The hierarchy preference is robust to $\Lambda$CDM, but is contingent on the dark energy model."

3. **Oscillation Likelihood Simplification:** You are currently using a product of Gaussians for oscillation parameters. This is acceptable, but ensure you are using the *full* NuFIT 6.0 covariance matrix (including correlations between $\Delta m^2_{21}$ and $\Delta m^2_{3\ell}$). If you are using independent Gaussians, you are likely overestimating the precision of the oscillation constraint, which artificially inflates the Bayes factor. Verify if the NuFIT 6.0 $\Delta \chi^2$ is being applied as a constant or if you are marginalizing over the oscillation parameters. Marginalization is preferred to avoid "double counting" the oscillation data.

4. **Double-Beta Decay Overclaim:** You state that a non-detection in upcoming experiments is the "most statistically probable outcome." This is a strong claim. You should qualify this by noting that this assumes the $\Lambda$CDM model is correct. If the true cosmology allows for higher $\Sigma m_\nu$ (e.g., via modified gravity or different neutrino physics), the $m_{\beta\beta}$ posterior would shift. Frame this as a "conditional prediction" rather than a definitive forecast.

5. **Missed Opportunity - The "Feldman-Cousins" Discrepancy:** You show that your truncated Gaussian fit and the FC profile likelihood are similar. However, the FC approach is frequentist and the Bayesian approach is inherently prior-dependent. You should add a small section explaining why the Bayesian evidence is *more* appropriate here than the frequentist profile likelihood, specifically focusing on how the Bayesian framework naturally incorporates the "Occam's Razor" penalty for the IH's required fine-tuning of mass splittings.

6. **Actionable Improvement for Future Iterations:** The current analysis is complete enough for a paper. Do not add more models. Instead, focus the next iteration on a "Stress Test": calculate the Bayes factor as a function of the *central value* of the cosmological constraint ($\mu_0$). Show at what value of $\mu_0$ the evidence for NH drops below $K=10$. This will provide the community with a "breaking point" for the hierarchy preference, which is more scientifically valuable than just reporting the current result.
