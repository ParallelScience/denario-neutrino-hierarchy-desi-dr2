### 8. Philosophical and Mathematical Analysis of Prior Choice

#### 8.1. The Role of Priors in Bayesian Model Comparison
In Bayesian model comparison, the Bayes factor $K = P(D|\text{NH}) / P(D|\text{IH})$ quantifies the relative evidence for the Normal Hierarchy (NH) versus the Inverted Hierarchy (IH). Unlike parameter estimation, where the effect of the prior often diminishes with highly informative data, the marginal likelihood (or Bayesian evidence) remains intrinsically dependent on the prior volume. The evidence is the average of the likelihood over the prior distribution; thus, models that require fine-tuning or occupy a smaller volume of the prior parameter space are naturally penalized. This automatic Occam's razor effect necessitates a rigorous justification for the chosen prior frameworks. In this study, we contrast two fundamentally different approaches: the physically motivated hierarchical SJPV prior and the objective, information-theoretic HS prior.

#### 8.2. The SJPV Prior: Exchangeability and Geometric Volume
The SJPV prior, introduced by Jimenez et al. (2022), is grounded in the physical assumption that the three neutrino mass eigenstates $(m_1, m_2, m_3)$ are generated by the same underlying physical mechanism. Consequently, they are treated as exchangeable random variables drawn from a common log-normal distribution:
<code> P(\log m_i | \mu, \sigma) \sim \mathcal{N}(\log \mu, \sigma) </code>
To maintain objectivity regarding the specific scale and variance of this mass generation mechanism, the hyperparameters $\mu$ and $\sigma$ are marginalized over broad, uniform hyperpriors: $\pi(\log \mu) \sim \mathcal{U}(\log(5\times 10^{-4}), \log(0.3))$ and $\pi(\log \sigma) \sim \mathcal{U}(\log(5\times 10^{-3}), \log(20))$.

This hierarchical structure induces a profound geometric volume effect in the parameter space. The NH allows for a natural hierarchical mass spectrum ($m_1 \ll m_2 \ll m_3$) or a quasi-degenerate spectrum. In contrast, the IH strictly requires the two heaviest mass eigenstates to be nearly degenerate ($m_1 \simeq m_2 \gg m_3$) to satisfy the observed solar and atmospheric mass-squared splittings. In the hyperparameter space of $(\mu, \sigma)$, generating two nearly degenerate heavy masses and one light mass is statistically much less probable than generating one heavy mass and two lighter masses.

As demonstrated in our prior volume analysis (Step 4), this requirement severely restricts the accessible prior volume for the IH. The numerical integration reveals that the SJPV prior inherently favors the NH by a substantial factor even before cosmological data is applied. For the baseline DESI DR2 constraints, the base Bayes factor (excluding the $\Delta\chi^2$ from the oscillation global fit) under the SJPV prior is $K_{\text{base}} \approx 484.5$. This strong preference is a direct manifestation of the geometric volume penalty: the IH is penalized because it requires a fine-tuned, unnatural mass spectrum under the assumption of exchangeability.

#### 8.3. The HS Prior: Fisher Information and Objective Reference Priors
To ensure that the preference for the NH is not solely an artifact of the exchangeability assumption, we employ the HS prior as a conservative, objective alternative. The HS prior is constructed following the Bernardo-Berger framework for reference priors, which seeks to maximize the influence of the data and minimize the information injected by the prior.

In the context of neutrino masses, the most direct observables from oscillation experiments are the mass-squared splittings $\phi = \Delta m^2_{21}$ and $\psi = \Delta m^2_{3\ell}$. If we treat the sum of the masses $\Sigma$ as the third independent parameter, the natural, non-informative prior in the space of these observables is a flat prior, $P(\phi, \psi, \Sigma) \propto 1$. This reflects a state of maximum ignorance regarding the parameters directly constrained by experiments.

To compute the Bayesian evidence, we must transform this flat prior into the space of the individual mass eigenstates $(m_L, m_M, m_H)$. This transformation is governed by the Jacobian determinant. The relationships are:
<code> \Sigma = m_L + m_M + m_H </code>
<code> \phi = m_M^2 - m_L^2 </code>
<code> \psi = m_H^2 - m_L^2 </code>
The Jacobian matrix $J$ for the transformation from $(\phi, \psi, \Sigma)$ to $(m_L, m_M, m_H)$ is:
<code> J = \begin{vmatrix} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial m_L} & \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial m_M} & \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial m_H} \\ \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial m_L} & \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial m_M} & \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial m_H} \\ \frac{\partial \Sigma}{\partial m_L} & \frac{\partial \Sigma}{\partial m_M} & \frac{\partial \Sigma}{\partial m_H} \end{vmatrix} = \begin{vmatrix} -2m_L & 2m_M & 0 \\ -2m_L & 0 & 2m_H \\ 1 & 1 & 1 \end{vmatrix} </code>
Evaluating the determinant yields:
<code> |J| = 1 \cdot (4m_M m_H) - 1 \cdot (-4m_L m_H) + 1 \cdot (4m_L m_M) = 4(m_L m_M + m_L m_H + m_M m_H) </code>
Thus, the HS reference prior is defined as:
<code> P_{\text{HS}}(m_L, m_M, m_H) \propto m_L m_M + m_L m_H + m_M m_H </code>
This Jacobian prior acts as the objective reference prior. Because it is derived directly from the Fisher information matrix of the oscillation observables, it does not impose a geometric penalty on the IH for requiring degenerate heavy masses. Consequently, the base Bayes factor under the HS prior is significantly lower, $K_{\text{base}} \approx 22.0$, which closely mirrors the result obtained using a naive flat prior on $\Sigma m_\nu$ ($K_{\text{base}} \approx 22.0$). Under the HS framework, the preference for the NH is driven almost entirely by the cosmological likelihood truncating the parameter space near the IH minimum mass threshold ($\Sigma_{\text{min}}^{\text{IH}} \approx 0.099$ eV).

#### 8.4. Implications for DESI DR2 Constraints
The contrast between the SJPV and HS priors highlights the dual nature of the evidence for the Normal Hierarchy. The new DESI DR2 baseline constraint ($\Sigma m_\nu < 0.0642$ eV at 95% CL) is in severe tension with the IH minimum mass.

When evaluated through the lens of the SJPV prior, the combination of the cosmological tension and the geometric volume penalty results in an overwhelmingly decisive total Bayes factor of $K_{\text{full}} \approx 10231$. This indicates that if one accepts the physical premise that neutrino masses share a common generation mechanism, the IH is effectively ruled out.

However, even if one rejects this physical premise and adopts the maximally agnostic, objective HS prior, the total Bayes factor remains $K_{\text{full}} \approx 464.6$. This value still far exceeds the threshold for "decisive" evidence ($K > 100$) on the Jeffreys scale. The implications are profound: the preference for the Normal Hierarchy is no longer dependent on prior choice. The DESI DR2 constraints are now sufficiently tight that the data alone, independent of geometric volume arguments, decisively favors the Normal Hierarchy. The 2022 methodology correctly foresaw this outcome, but the DESI DR2 data has transitioned the conclusion from prior-dependent to robustly data-driven.
