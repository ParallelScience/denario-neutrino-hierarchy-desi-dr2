**Title**: Decisive Bayesian Evidence for the Normal Neutrino Mass Hierarchy from DESI DR2

**Description**: This study quantifies the statistical preference for the Normal Hierarchy (NH) by applying the Jimenez et al. (2022) Bayesian evidence framework to the DESI DR2 cosmological chains, specifically deconvolving the flat prior inherent in the public Cobaya chains to isolate the true cosmological likelihood $P(D_{\text{cosmo}} | \Sigma)$. We compute Bayes factors $K = P(D|NH)/P(D|IH)$ using both SJPV hierarchical logarithmic and HS objective priors, rigorously incorporating NuFIT 6.0 oscillation constraints and fully marginalizing over Majorana CP-violating phases to produce a statistically sound posterior interval for the effective mass $m_{\beta\beta}$. To address the model-dependency of the hierarchy preference, we contrast the evidence strength derived from the $\Lambda$CDM baseline against the $w_0w_a$CDM extension, where the degradation of $K$ serves as a critical measure of the sensitivity of the hierarchy constraint to dark energy parameterization. The analysis includes a formal mathematical treatment of prior choice, contrasting the geometric volume effects of the SJPV and HS frameworks. We reconstruct the historical evolution of $K$ from 2002 to 2025 to validate the predictive power of the 2022 methodology against the increasing tension between cosmological data and the IH minimum mass threshold. This comprehensive evaluation provides a definitive, data-driven assessment of the neutrino mass hierarchy, culminating in robust projections for next-generation $0\nu\beta\beta$ experiments.
